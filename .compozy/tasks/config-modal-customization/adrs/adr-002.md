# ADR-002: Guided Control Center Product Approach for Config Modal Customization

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-05-29

## Context

Config Modal Customization needs to solve two product problems at once: Another ADE lacks a dedicated settings surface, and prospective switchers from Cursor and VS Code expect core personalization controls such as agent defaults, theme choice, and shortcut customization.

Research and brainstorming established that the primary product outcome is stronger user control over agent behavior, the primary V1 audience is switchers from mature developer tools, the preferred agent strategy is curated expansion rather than a fully open catalog, and the success signal that matters most is a higher share of sessions launched from saved defaults instead of repeated manual setup.

The remaining decision was how broad and opinionated the first release should be: a full guided bundle, a narrower agent-first rollout, or a more open-ended power-user surface.

## Decision

The PRD will follow a **Guided Control Center** approach.

V1 will ship a single dedicated config modal that covers the complete user-facing bundle selected during brainstorming: curated agent expansion, editable default commands for built-in agents, high-quality light and dark themes, and customization of the most important workflow shortcuts.

The product story will still be led by agent control and session defaults, but the release should feel complete enough for switchers from Cursor and VS Code to adopt Another ADE as a daily driver. The experience should stay curated, safe, and focused rather than becoming an open-ended settings platform.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Agent-First Rollout

- **Description**: Launch agent/default-command control and curated new agents first, then add themes and shortcuts later.
- **Pros**: Strong focus on the top user outcome, faster learning loop, and smaller initial scope.
- **Cons**: Risks feeling incomplete to switchers, weakens parity with mature tools, and delays parts of the bundle users said make the release feel complete.
- **Why rejected**: The chosen audience expects a more complete customization story at launch, and the user explicitly prioritized the full bundle over a staged release.

### Alternative 2: Open Power Studio

- **Description**: Launch the full bundle with broader user-defined agent flexibility and a more advanced power-user settings experience from day one.
- **Pros**: Maximizes flexibility, appeals to experiment-heavy users, and offers a stronger power-user narrative.
- **Cons**: Increases product complexity, raises trust and usability risks, and makes the first release harder to keep focused.
- **Why rejected**: The product needs a guided, credible daily-driver experience before it expands into a more open-ended power-user platform.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Delivers a more complete and competitive first impression for switchers from mature developer tools.
- Keeps agent control at the center while still covering the visible personalization gaps users notice immediately.
- Improves the odds that users will start more sessions from saved defaults instead of repeating setup work.

### Negative

- Broadens the initial feature scope beyond a narrow agent-only rollout.
- Requires strong prioritization so the modal does not become an unfocused collection of settings.
- Creates higher user expectations for polish across themes, shortcuts, and agent management at launch.

### Risks

- **Scope dilution**: too many secondary controls could weaken the primary story of agent control. Mitigation: keep the modal organized around agent defaults and daily workflow fit.
- **Perceived incompleteness**: if shortcut or theme support feels shallow, users may still compare the product unfavorably with mature tools. Mitigation: focus on the most visible, most frequently used customization paths.
- **Trust erosion**: if command customization feels opaque or risky, users may avoid the feature. Mitigation: maintain explicit, user-visible control and clear built-in versus custom states.

## Implementation Notes

- This ADR defines product scope and experience shape only.
- The PRD should preserve a curated, guided feel and avoid implying an open agent marketplace, cross-device sync, or exhaustive customization in V1.
